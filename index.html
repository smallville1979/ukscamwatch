13
🪪 Most Dangerous

Identity &
Account Scams

These don't just cost you money. They can cost you your benefits, your bank account, and your freedom. These are the ones that ruin lives.

CRITICAL 🪪

KYC Passport / ID Scam

Someone asks you to send a photo of your passport, driving licence, or bank statement. They say it's for "verification" to unlock a payment or referral bonus. It's not. They're selling your identity or using it to open fraudulent accounts.

Your ID in someone else's hands = identity theft, fraud charges, and a destroyed credit file. Forever.
CRITICAL 💳

Account Buying / Selling

Someone offers you £50-£200 for your bank account details or to use your account to receive money. They say it's "just for transfers." It's money laundering. When police investigate — it's YOUR account. YOU get arrested. YOU lose your benefits.

Selling your account = money mule = criminal conviction. People are going to jail for this right now.
CRITICAL 🎮

Discord Account Buying

Paying or getting paid for Discord, Reddit, or social media accounts. Often linked to crypto scams, fraud networks, and bot farms. Your account gets used to scam other people — then traced back to you.

Platform accounts tied to your identity. When fraud happens through them — you're liable.
CRITICAL 🌍

Nigerian / West African Account Hacking

Facebook friend requests from attractive strangers, long conversations building "trust," then requests to click a link, log in somewhere, or share a code. Your account gets hijacked and used to scam your friends and family.

Never click links from new Facebook friends. Never share login codes. If it feels off — it is.
💸 Money Scams

Fake Money
& "Free Cash"

If someone's offering you free money on Facebook, Reddit or Discord — read this first. Every single one of these has a catch that costs you more than you gain.

HIGH RISK 💰

"Free £5 / £10 / £20" Facebook Posts

Posts saying "comment INFO for free £5" with 13+ comments. Nobody gets £5. You get added to a WhatsApp group, sold a course, or harvested for data. The engagement is fake or desperate people who never received anything.

If free money required a comment — it's data harvesting or a funnel into something worse.
HIGH RISK 📱

eBay Selling Tax Trap

People sell stuff on eBay thinking it's just pocket money. Since 2024 HMRC gets automatic reports if you sell over £1,000/year. People getting surprise tax bills, penalties, and investigations — years later when they've forgotten.

Not technically a scam — but nobody tells you. Sell over £1,000/year on eBay? Register as self-employed or declare it.
HIGH RISK 🤖

Outlier AI / "Work From Home AI" Traps

Platforms like Outlier pay you in USD as a US contractor. Sounds great. Then a US tax form arrives. You owe IRS money. UK tax implications too. What looked like extra income becomes a cross-border tax nightmare nobody warned you about.

Any platform paying in USD as a "contractor" = potential US tax liability. Ask before you start.
HIGH RISK 📊

Reddit Loan Groups

"Borrow £100, repay £120 next week." Real people lending to strangers on Reddit. The lenders are often scammers who take your personal info then disappear. The borrowers often never repay and vanish. Nobody wins. Everyone gets burned.

Reddit peer lending = unregulated, unprotected, almost always a scam on one or both sides.
MEDIUM 💡

Marketing "Secret Sauce" Agencies

"Get to the top of Google Maps for £1.90/day!" It's just a basic Google Business Profile setup. Takes 20 minutes and costs nothing. They charge £150+ setup and monthly fees for something you can do yourself in an afternoon for free.

Google Business Profile is free. Any agency charging £100+ just to set it up is ripping you off.
MEDIUM 🧠

AI Prompt Selling

Someone sells you a £10-£50 "prompt pack" for ChatGPT or Claude. Fancy PDF, lots of hype. The prompts inside are things you could have typed yourself in 30 seconds. AI will literally write better prompts for free if you just ask it to.

Ask any AI: "Write me a prompt to do X." It will do it. For free. Instantly. Every time.
👤 Personal Scams

Personal &
Social Scams

These target you personally — through loneliness, desperation, or just being a decent person. Recognise the scripts before they run them on you.

HIGH RISK 📸

Nude Selling / Sextortion

Woman adds you on Snapchat or Instagram. Builds a tiny bit of chat. Then: "I sell nudes, want to buy?" Or they share something first — then demand payment or they'll send screenshots to your contacts. The "woman" is often not who they claim.

Never pay. Never engage. Block and report immediately. Paying once guarantees they come back for more.
HIGH RISK ⛽

Snapchat "Petrol / Emergency" Scam

Stranger adds you, tells a believable sob story — stranded, need petrol, just £20-£40, will pay back. Story has holes if you look. Once you send — they're gone. Or pivot to selling nudes. Same script, different opener every time.

Strangers asking for money via Snapchat / Instagram are running a script. The story is always slightly off.
MEDIUM 📧

Dodgy Texts & Emails

"Your parcel is held." "Your bank account has been suspended." "HMRC owes you a refund." All fake. All trying to get you to click a link and enter your details. The links look real. The websites look real. They're not.

Never click links in unexpected texts or emails. Go directly to the official website by typing it yourself.

⚙️ How the Snapchat / Social Scam Actually Works — The Full Script

1

Random add. Attractive profile picture. No mutual friends. Adds you out of nowhere.

2

Small talk. "Hey how are you?" Friendly. Normal. Building just enough rapport.

3

The story. Stranded. Petrol. Sick relative. Lost purse. Always urgent. Always just £20-£40. Always "I'll pay you back."

4

If you hesitate — they pivot to nude selling, or get aggressive, or disappear and try again next week with a new profile.

5

If you pay — they're gone. Or they come back. Because you paid once which means you might pay again.

💔 Romance Scams

Catfish &
Romance Scams

They target loneliness. They're patient. They'll talk to you for weeks before asking for anything. And they're running 50 conversations at the same time as yours. This one ruins lives.

❤️ NEVER SEND MONEY TO SOMEONE YOU HAVEN'T MET IN PERSON. EVER. NOT ONCE. NOT EVEN £10.
CRITICAL 😍

The Hi-Vis Catfish — Gay Groups

"Hi guys I'm new here looking for a long term relationship message me" — anonymous account, stolen photo of a good looking young guy, dodgy link. Posted in gay singles groups, silver daddies groups, lonely hearts groups. 29 reactions in an hour. Every single one is fake or a victim.

Anonymous participant + attractive photo + no post history + weird link = Nigerian romance scammer. Report and move on.
CRITICAL 📱

The Long Game — Weeks of Chat

They add you, chat every day, say good morning, remember things you told them, make you feel special. Then after 2-4 weeks — an "emergency." Hospital bill. Stuck abroad. Package held at customs. Just needs £200. Will pay back immediately. They won't.

The longer they've been nice to you — the bigger the ask will be. Kindness is investment. The return they want is your money.
CRITICAL 🌍

The Phone Farm Operation

This isn't one person sitting at home. These are organised operations — rooms full of people in West Africa running 50-100 romantic conversations simultaneously from multiple phones. It's a 9-5 job. Scripts are shared. Photos are stolen from random people's Instagram. You are a number on a spreadsheet.

If they can't video call, won't meet, camera is always "broken" — they are not who they say they are. Full stop.
HIGH RISK 🔗

The Spoo.me / Shortlink Trap

Posts with spoo.me, bit.ly, or other link shorteners that hide the destination. Could go anywhere — data harvesting pages, fake WhatsApp redirects, malware downloads, fake login pages. The anonymous post with a short link in gay groups is textbook Nigerian scammer setup.

Never click shortened links from anonymous accounts or people you don't know. Check where links go at checkshorturl.com first.
HIGH RISK 💸

Western Union / Gift Card Asks

Once the "emergency" comes — they'll ask for Western Union, MoneyGram, iTunes gift cards, Google Play cards, or bank transfer. Why? Because these can't be reversed. Once sent — gone forever. No bank protection. No refund. Nothing.

Any request for gift cards or Western Union as payment is a scam. No exceptions. Legitimate people don't need iTunes cards for emergencies.
HIGH RISK 📸

Sextortion — The Photo Trap

They persuade you to share intimate photos or get on video. Then the tone changes completely. "Pay me or I send these to everyone you know." They often already have your Facebook friends list. This is organised blackmail. Victims have taken their own lives over this.

Never share intimate images with anyone you haven't met in person. If this happens to you — do NOT pay. Report to Action Fraud immediately.

💔 The Full Romance Scam Script — So You Recognise It

1

The approach. Attractive stranger adds you or messages out of nowhere. Profile looks real but is recently created or sparse.

2

Building trust. Daily messages. Good morning texts. Remembers everything. Makes you feel seen and understood. This goes on for days or weeks.

3

The block. Can never video call. Camera broken. Works on an oil rig. Serving abroad in the military. Always a reason they can't appear on screen.

4

Falling for it. You start to develop feelings. You defend them to friends. You make excuses for the video call thing. This is working as designed.

5

The emergency. Hospital. Customs fee. Flight home. Stuck abroad. Just needs £200-£500. Will pay back immediately. Sounds completely believable.

6

If you pay — they come back. Emergency after emergency. Until you stop. Then they disappear. Or they threaten you if you have photos.

7

If you refuse — they often get aggressive, guilt-trip you, or vanish immediately. Real partners don't vanish when you say you can't send money.

🚨 If this is happening to you right now:

Stop all contact immediately. Do not send money. Do not send photos. Report the profile on whatever platform you're using. Report to Action Fraud. Tell someone you trust. You are not stupid — these people are professionals who do this full time. It happens to smart, educated people every single day.

🚩 Universal Red Flags

If You See
Any of These —
Walk Away

Doesn't matter what the offer is. If any of these apply — it's a scam. No exceptions.

They need your ID, passport, or bank details to "release" money
They contact you first out of nowhere
The story doesn't quite add up
They need money before you get paid
They want payment via PayPal Friends, Cash App, or bank transfer
They won't video call or their camera is "broken"
They're unusually complimentary very quickly
They get aggressive when you question anything
The group admin won't allow links to outside resources
Everyone in the comments says "Info" or "Yes please"
The opportunity involves receiving money into your account for someone else
"No time wasters" in the post — classic pressure tactic

The golden rule: If someone you've never met in person is offering you money, asking for money, or asking for your personal details — assume it's a scam until proven otherwise. Real opportunities don't need to pressure you. Real people don't need your passport. Real jobs don't ask you to receive money for them.
